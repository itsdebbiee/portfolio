Word Bank

For a Human-Computer Interaction class, my team and I decided to build a mobile web app to help French learners find songs to listen to that are close to their language level and in turn, help them learn that language in a more colloquial sense. What resulted was an application that is similar to Rap Genius and Duolingo, with an option for users to save phrases that they wanted to practice more. Our goal is to make language learning a fun and convenient activity that can be done anytime, anywhere.

In this project, I took on the role as one of the coders, since I had the most experience with HTML, CSS and jQuery. I helped my other teammate who also had some coding experience with getting the functionality of the application working, but my biggest and most enjoyable role was doing the end UI design.

Storyboards

We created storyboards as part of the process we used to figure out what exactly our users wanted and would need in a situation. This was a helpful way of putting down onto paper an actual real life scenario that our users would use our web application for. Our storyboard depicted the user being frustrated at not being able to find songs that they could understand when trying to practice their French - however, by using an application with preset song difficulties, they were able to practice their French successfully and ace their final exam.

Paper Prototype

After the storyboard, we created a paper prototype with moving parts to simulate what our product would be like and to test for any breakdowns in the user flow. Quick heuristic evaluations were done by our peers and feedback was provided to help us decide which features to keep or which features to omit in the next design iteration. This was a step that was tremendously helpful to us in weeding out bad design decisions first before we committed more time into coding anything.

Functional Prototype

With the initial kinks and breakdowns identified from the heuristic evaluations, we proceeded create our first functional prototype. This time around, we conducted user studies and interviews with real users from our user group to further iron out more problems. We found that users were confused about the icons in our navigation bar and thought that the French flag we had in the middle as an icon for All Songs was a pause button instead. They also were confused that when they switched to a different page, the page would load at the bottom so sometimes there would be no content - so many thought they broke the program. These were just a few of the many problems that they encountered and after taking note of what areas were confusing, we were able to make our final prototype a lot better. The final prototype showcased above is the result of many iterations of testing and overall, was a great hands-on experience.
