cognection

In a project-based class focused on teaching us how to use the process of rapid contextual design, I worked with a team of seven other students to create an application bringing together the social aspects of athletic activities such as basketball, soccer, etc. Our goal was to help facilitate planning and communication as well as making it more convenient for people to find games to play and introduce a social aspect to these activities by integrating their Facebook friends.

We started by identifying and conducting contextual interviews on those in our target population, of which included people who were on sports teams, people who do recreational activities in groups, and people who go to the gym. Through this process, we discovered that our target users used a variety of communication platforms to coordinate these meet-ups. Oftentimes, these methods were difficult and time consuming, and would occasionally result in failed plans if not enough people responded or showed up.

Affinity Wall

Creating the affinity wall was really insightful. By doing this, we were able to see breakdowns that weren't as apparent to us before, much more clearly. The wall also allowed us to see areas for design inspirations and opportunities as well. Overall, it gave us a great picture of what areas we needed to focus on and what features we ultimately needed to include in our final design to fully address the needs of our target users.

Flowcharts

The next step in our process was to take all of this information in our affinity wall and consolidate it into something more readable. We found that there were two types of users: initiators (those who took the initiative to set up and organize events with others) and followers (those who often looked for activities to participate in). These models are a visual representation of the actions that individuals belonging to each group take, as well as their roles and responsibilities in the situation.

Low-fidelity Prototypes

After analyzing the data that we collected, we created two low-fidelity prototypes to convey our design ideas and plans using Axure. In these prototypes, the user can see what activities are happening when, how many people are attending each activity, and which of their friends are going to each event.
